CS1102 - Course Project - 2025 / 2026 Semester B

Topic: AI-Driven Detection of Synthetic Identity Fraud in Consumer Lending

Case Study: Real-World Application of AI Fraud Detection

Project Overview

  • Scenario: Consumer lending platform facing growing synthetic identity fraud losses
  • Traditional rule-based system was failing to detect coordinated "piggybacking" fraud rings

AI Solution Implemented

  • Hybrid approach combining:
    • Graph Neural Networks (GNN) to map relational patterns between applications
    • Anomaly detection models to identify unusual temporal/behavioral signals
  • Key features leveraged:
    • Time-aware application patterns (same IP/device in short windows)
    • Credit history growth velocity (fast "piggybacking" before default)
    • Hidden connections between synthetic identities

Outcomes & Measurable Results

  • 35% increase in detection of coordinated synthetic fraud rings
  • 20% reduction in false positive rates compared to the old rule-based system
  • Early detection of emerging fraud tactics, reducing write-off losses by an estimated $2.1M annually
