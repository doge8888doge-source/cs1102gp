CS1102 - Course Project - 2025 / 2026 Semester B

Topic: AI-Driven Detection of Synthetic Identity Fraud in Consumer Lending

Measurable benefits and evidence

  • Higher detection of coordinated fraud: studies and industry reports show graph approaches detect patterns that traditional systems miss; synthetic identity losses remain large, motivating graph methods. (Industry loss estimate cited: $6.8B; rule systems miss many emerging patterns.)
  • Faster triage: prioritized alerts (Precision@K) reduce investigator workload and time-to-action.

Risks, trade-offs, and mitigations

  • False positives and customer friction: tune thresholds; use human-in-the-loop review and explainable subgraph extracts. Calibrate by Precision@K and FPR.
  • Bias and fairness: ensure demographic proxies are not used as shortcuts; audit disparate FPRs and apply threshold adjustments.
  • Operational complexity: requires data pipelines for entity resolution and privacy-compliant data handling; start with pilot on high-risk segments.

Practical next steps for a lender

  1. Assemble entity graph from recent applications and device/IP logs.
  2. Seed labels with known fraud cases; run supervised GNN + anomaly detector.
  3. Evaluate using Precision@K, Recall, and FPR; measure investigator workload.
  4. Deploy hybrid scoring with human review and explainability outputs.
